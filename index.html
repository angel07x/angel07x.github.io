ATmega128

8-bit MCU


The AVR-based microcomputer learns microcomputer programming by using the ATmega128-based practical board. To this end, understand the overall internal structure and function of the ATmega128 microcomputer, and practice implementing a control program to utilize a 2x16 character LCD, a representative example using GPIO. In addition, in order to utilize the special functions implemented inside the microcomputer, such as analog/digital converters, timers/counters, and USART, the functions of related registers, setting methods, and implementation of control programs using them are practiced.

[Learned]

  • The internal structure and function of the microcomputer
  • The functions of the registers and how to set them up
  • Understanding the operating principles of the control program for external interrupt utilization, etc.

Circuit Experiment

Practical Application of Circuit Theory


Through experiments, I gained a deeper understanding of the fundamental principles of both DC and AC circuits. By applying theoretical knowledge to real circuits, I was able to grasp key electrical laws and the operating principles of electronic components. Additionally, during the experiments, I directly operated measurement equipment such as oscilloscopes, multimeters, and function generators to measure voltage, current, and frequency responses. This enabled me to analyze signal characteristics and verify circuit operation, thereby enhancing my circuit analysis and design skills and developing the ability to understand and resolve issues in practical circuits.

[Experiments Conducted]

  • DC Circuit Analysis: Experiments on Ohm’s law, series and parallel resistor networks, and Kirchhoff’s voltage and current laws.
  • AC Circuit Analysis: Experiments on RLC series and parallel resonance circuits, differentiator and integrator circuits, and low-pass, high-pass, and band-pass filters.
  • Circuit Theorems and Laws: Experiments on the superposition principle, Thevenin’s theorem, Norton’s theorem, and maximum power transfer conditions.
  • Semiconductor Device Experiments: Analysis of various diodes, bipolar junction transistors (BJTs), and MOSFETs to understand their principles and characteristics.
  • Amplifier and Op-Amp Applications: Analysis of different types of amplifiers and their characteristics, and experiments using operational amplifiers in various circuit configurations.
